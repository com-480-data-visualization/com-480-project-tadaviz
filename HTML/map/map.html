In this section, you will be able to visualize data for each country with respect to the chosen criterion.
The criterions are the following :
  • Number of respondents : Shows the number of respondents in the dataset, at the scale of a country.
  • Dominant personality
    • : Shows the dominant personality trait of a given country, accompanied by a radar graph which shows the distribution of the scores with respect to each personality trait. The pink polygon represents the mean distribution at the scale of the world. A min-max normalization was applied to these distributions, which explains that for some countries, their score for a given personality trait is equal to 0.
  • Happiness level
    • : Shows the happiness level at the scale of a country.
  • Sensitive trait
    • : Shows the sensitive trait at the scale of a country. For a given country, the sensitive trait corresponds to the personality trait associated to the category of questions for which, in average, the respondents took the most time to answer.
